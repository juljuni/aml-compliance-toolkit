# AML/CTF Compliance Requirements for Australian Accounting Firms

**Last Updated:** February 14, 2026
**Effective Date:** July 1, 2026 (Tranche 2 reforms)
**Registration Deadline:** March 31, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

From July 1, 2026, accounting firms providing certain designated professional services will become "reporting entities" under the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing Act 2006 (AML/CTF Act). This guide outlines the mandatory obligations for small accounting practices.

---

## 1. TRIGGERS FOR AML OBLIGATIONS

### 1.1 Designated Services (Table 6)

Your accounting firm will have AML obligations if you provide **any** of the following designated services:

**Professional Services that Trigger Obligations:**
- **Assisting** with the formation, operation, or management of a body corporate (company)
- **Assisting** with the formation, operation, or management of a trust or legal arrangement
- **Assisting** with the buying or selling of a business entity
- **Assisting** with real estate transactions

**Key Definition of "Assisting":**
- Your actions must be **sufficiently linked** to the outcome of the designated service
- Your assistance must **directly advance** the relevant transaction
- Merely influencing how a customer proceeds or providing general advice is **NOT sufficient**
- The service applies whether the entity is existing or only "proposed" (in formation)

### 1.2 Services NOT Typically Captured

The following services typically do NOT trigger AML obligations:
- Tax return preparation only
- Basic bookkeeping services
- Financial statement preparation
- General tax advice
- Payroll services (unless connected to entity formation)

### 1.3 Threshold Amounts

- **No minimum threshold** for most professional services
- **$10,000 threshold** applies only to precious metals/stones transactions
- Professional services are regulated **regardless of fee amount** or transaction value

### 1.4 Geographical Link

You must have a **geographical link to Australia** for obligations to apply:
- You provide the service in Australia, OR
- You provide the service to a customer in Australia, OR
- The service relates to Australian entities/transactions

## 2. MANDATORY COMPLIANCE OBLIGATIONS

### 2.1 Registration & Enrollment

**MUST DO:**
- Enroll with AUSTRAC by **March 31, 2026**
- Obtain AUSTRAC registration before providing designated services from July 1, 2026

**Penalties for Non-Enrollment:**
- **$18,780 per day** for firms (body corporate)
- **$3,756 per day** for individuals
- Penalties accrue daily until enrolled

### 2.2 AML/CTF Program

**MUST HAVE by July 1, 2026:**

1. **Part A - Risk Assessment**
   - Identify and assess money laundering (ML) risks
   - Identify and assess terrorism financing (TF) risks
   - Identify and assess proliferation financing (PF) risks
   - Document risk assessment methodology
   - Review and update risk assessment regularly

2. **Part B - AML/CTF Program**
   - Policies addressing identified risks
   - Procedures for customer due diligence
   - Systems and controls to manage risks
   - Transaction monitoring procedures
   - Reporting procedures for suspicious matters
   - Record keeping procedures
   - Employee screening procedures
   - Training program for all staff

3. **Compliance Officer**
   - Nominate an AML/CTF Compliance Officer
   - Officer must have sufficient authority and resources
   - Officer is responsible for program implementation and monitoring

### 2.3 Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

**2.3.1 Initial CDD - Before Providing Service**

**MUST establish on reasonable grounds:**
- Customer's identity (full name, date of birth, residential address)
- Customer type (individual, company, trust, partnership, etc.)
- Nature and purpose of the business relationship
- Beneficial owners (individuals owning 25%+ of the entity)
- Whether customer is a Politically Exposed Person (PEP)

**MUST verify using reliable and independent documentation:**
- Government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport)
- Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement - within 3 months)
- Company registration documents (ASIC extract for companies)
- Trust deed or partnership agreement
- Beneficial owner identification documents

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Documents must be **current** (passports can be up to 2 years past expiry)
- Can use electronic verification services
- Must document verification method used
- Must keep copies or detailed records of documents sighted

**2.3.2 Simplified CDD**

Available for **lower-risk** customers (subject to your risk assessment):
- Reduced documentation requirements
- Faster verification process
- Must still establish identity and beneficial ownership

**2.3.3 Enhanced CDD**

Required for **higher-risk** customers:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Customers from high-risk jurisdictions
- Complex ownership structures
- Unusual transaction patterns
- Additional documentation required
- Senior management approval needed

### 2.4 Ongoing Customer Due Diligence (OCDD)

**Effective:** March 31, 2026

**MUST do:**
- **Review** customer KYC information at frequency appropriate to their ML/TF risk
- **Update** customer information when changes detected or doubts arise
- **Reverify** customer identity when risk increases or doubts exist
- **Monitor** transactions for unusual or suspicious activity

**Review Frequency:**
- Set in your AML/CTF policies based on risk
- Higher-risk customers: more frequent reviews (e.g., annually)
- Lower-risk customers: less frequent reviews (e.g., every 3-5 years)

**Triggers for Immediate Review:**
- Doubts about adequacy or truth of previous information
- Material change in customer circumstances
- Unusual transaction activity
- Customer behavior inconsistent with risk profile
- Media reports or adverse information about customer

### 2.5 Transitioning Existing Customers

**Pre-commencement customers** (clients before July 1, 2026):
- **3-year grace period** to complete full CDD (until July 1, 2029)
- Must complete simplified "know your customer" (KYC) information initially
- Must prioritize higher-risk customers for full CDD
- Must complete full CDD if triggered by specific events

**Prioritization approach:**
- Higher-risk customers: complete CDD within first year
- Medium-risk customers: complete CDD within second year
- Lower-risk customers: complete CDD by end of third year

## 3. EVIDENCE & DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED

### 3.1 For Individual Customers

**Primary Identity Documents (minimum 1):**
- Australian driver's license (with photo)
- Australian passport
- Foreign passport (with Australian visa)
- State/Territory ID card

**Secondary Documents (minimum 1 for address):**
- Utility bill (gas, electricity, water) - within 3 months
- Bank statement - within 3 months
- Rates notice - within 12 months
- Electoral roll extract

**For Beneficial Owners:**
- Same requirements as primary customer
- Must identify all individuals owning 25%+ directly or indirectly

### 3.2 For Company Customers

**Required information:**
- Full company name
- ACN/ARBN (Australian Company Number)
- Registered office address
- Principal place of business
- Nature of business activities

**Verification documents:**
- ASIC company extract (current, within 3 months)
- Constitution or trust deed (if applicable)

**Beneficial owners:**
- Identify all individuals owning 25%+ of shares
- Director information (if no beneficial owner identified)
- Same ID requirements as individuals

### 3.3 For Trust Customers

**Required information:**
- Trust name
- Trust type (family, unit, charitable, etc.)
- Date of establishment
- Jurisdiction of establishment

**Verification documents:**
- Trust deed
- Trustee details (individual or corporate)
- Beneficiary information

**Beneficial owners:**
- Settlor (if contributing more than initial settlement)
- Trustee(s)
- Beneficiaries with 25%+ beneficial interest
- Appointor/guardian (control persons)

### 3.4 Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)

**Must identify if customer is:**
- **Foreign PEP:** Holds prominent public position in foreign country
- **Domestic PEP:** Holds prominent public position in Australia
- **International organization PEP:** Senior role in international body

**Enhanced requirements for PEPs:**
- Senior management approval before establishing relationship
- Enhanced CDD measures
- More frequent ongoing monitoring
- Source of wealth verification
- Purpose of relationship documentation

## 4. RECORD KEEPING REQUIREMENTS

### 4.1 Types of Records to Keep

**Customer identification records:**
- All documents used to verify identity
- Copies of ID documents (or detailed descriptions)
- Verification method used (document inspection, electronic, etc.)
- Date verification completed
- Name of staff member who completed verification

**Transaction records:**
- All transactions for designated services
- Date, nature, and value of transaction
- Parties involved
- Purpose of transaction
- Any supporting documentation

**AML/CTF Program records:**
- Risk assessment documents
- Policies and procedures
- Training records
- Suspicious matter reports (SMRs)
- Compliance officer appointment
- Program reviews and updates

**Ongoing monitoring records:**
- Review dates and findings
- Updated information collected
- Risk rating changes
- Management decisions on customer relationships

### 4.2 Retention Periods

**7 years retention required for:**
- Customer identification records (from date relationship ends)
- Transaction records (from date of transaction)
- AML/CTF program documents (from date program ends/replaced)
- Training records (from date training provided)
- Suspicious matter reports (from date report submitted)

**Ongoing while in use:**
- Current risk assessment
- Current AML/CTF policies and procedures
- Current customer information

### 4.3 Storage Requirements

**MUST ensure records are:**
- **Secure:** Protected from unauthorized access, loss, or damage
- **Retrievable:** Can be accessed quickly when needed (AUSTRAC audits)
- **Readable:** In format that can be understood (English translation if needed)
- **Auditable:** Include metadata showing when created/modified

**Format options:**
- Electronic storage (secure cloud, encrypted databases)
- Hard copy (secure physical storage)
- Hybrid approach (both electronic and paper)

**Best practices:**
- Regular backups
- Access controls and permissions
- Audit trails of who accessed records
- Disaster recovery plan

## 5. ONGOING MONITORING FREQUENCY

### 5.1 Risk-Based Approach

Your AML/CTF program must specify monitoring frequency based on customer risk:

**Higher-Risk Customers (review annually or more frequently):**
- PEPs and their associates
- Customers from high-risk jurisdictions
- Complex ownership structures
- Cash-intensive businesses
- Customers with adverse media/information
- Unusual transaction patterns

**Medium-Risk Customers (review every 2-3 years):**
- Standard business customers
- Established relationships with no concerns
- Moderate transaction volumes
- No PEP connections

**Lower-Risk Customers (review every 3-5 years):**
- Long-standing customers with known history
- Simple structures (individuals, basic companies)
- Consistent, expected transaction patterns
- No red flags or concerns

### 5.2 Continuous Transaction Monitoring

**MUST monitor for:**
- Transactions inconsistent with customer profile
- Sudden changes in transaction volume or pattern
- Transactions with no apparent business purpose
- Transactions involving high-risk jurisdictions
- Structuring behavior (breaking up transactions to avoid thresholds)

**Monitoring methods:**
- Manual review for small firms
- Automated systems for larger firms
- Exception reports flagging unusual activity
- Regular review of all customer accounts

### 5.3 Event-Triggered Reviews

**MUST review immediately when:**
- Suspicious activity detected
- Material change in customer circumstances (new director, change of ownership)
- Customer requests unusual service
- Adverse media or information received
- Transaction significantly different from expected pattern
- Customer non-cooperative with information requests

## 6. REPORTING OBLIGATIONS

### 6.1 Suspicious Matter Reports (SMRs)

**MUST report within 24 hours if you have reasonable grounds to suspect:**
- Transaction involves proceeds of crime
- Transaction relates to terrorism financing
- Transaction relates to proliferation financing
- Customer is involved in money laundering

**SMR must include:**
- Customer details and identification
- Details of suspicious transaction(s)
- Reasons for suspicion (red flags observed)
- Any other relevant information

**Critical:**
- **Tipping off is prohibited** - Do NOT tell customer about SMR
- Penalty for tipping off: up to 2 years imprisonment or significant fines

### 6.2 Threshold Transaction Reports (TTRs)

**Generally NOT applicable to professional services**
- TTRs typically apply to cash transactions of $10,000+
- Most professional services do not involve large cash transactions
- If you do receive $10,000+ in physical cash, TTR required within 10 days

### 6.3 Reporting Timelines

- **SMRs:** Within 24 hours of forming suspicion (or as soon as practicable)
- **TTRs:** Within 10 business days of transaction (if applicable)
- **Late reporting:** May result in penalties

## 7. PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE

### 7.1 Civil Penalties

**Maximum civil penalties for body corporate:**
- **Serious breach:** 100,000 penalty units (currently **$37.8 million**)
- **Non-serious breach:** Variable based on breach type

**Individual penalties:**
- Generally 1/5 of corporate penalty
- Up to 20,000 penalty units for serious breach

**Examples of serious breaches:**
- Failure to have AML/CTF program
- Failure to conduct customer due diligence
- Failure to report suspicious matters
- Intentional or reckless non-compliance

### 7.2 Administrative Penalties

**Infringement notices (on-the-spot fines):**
- For less serious breaches
- Alternative to court proceedings
- Amounts vary by breach type

**Daily penalties for ongoing breaches:**
- Failure to enroll: $18,780/day (firms), $3,756/day (individuals)
- Penalties accrue until compliance achieved

### 7.3 Criminal Penalties

**For serious offenses:**
- Tipping off: Up to 2 years imprisonment
- Providing designated service while not enrolled: Criminal offense
- Intentional non-compliance with reporting obligations: Criminal offense

### 7.4 Other Consequences

**Reputational damage:**
- Public enforcement actions published by AUSTRAC
- Media coverage of breaches
- Loss of client confidence

**Operational consequences:**
- AUSTRAC enforcement actions (remedial directions)
- External audits required
- Increased regulatory scrutiny
- Potential business restrictions

### 7.5 Enforcement Considerations

**Factors affecting penalty amount:**
- Nature and extent of breach
- Level of intent or recklessness
- Harm caused to financial system
- Compliance history of entity
- Level of cooperation with AUSTRAC
- Steps taken to remediate breach
- Deterrence considerations

## 8. IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE

### March 31, 2026
- **DEADLINE:** Enroll with AUSTRAC
- **DEADLINE:** OCDD obligations begin for existing reporting entities
- **ACTION:** Have draft AML/CTF program ready

### July 1, 2026
- **EFFECTIVE:** Tranche 2 designated services commence
- **REQUIRED:** Must have complete AML/CTF program in place
- **REQUIRED:** Must conduct CDD before providing designated services
- **REQUIRED:** Must begin transaction monitoring
- **REQUIRED:** Must begin reporting suspicious matters

### July 1, 2026 - July 1, 2029
- **3-year transition period** for pre-existing customers
- Complete full CDD on existing clients (prioritize by risk)
- Review and update AML/CTF program regularly
- Train staff on obligations

### July 1, 2029
- **DEADLINE:** All pre-existing customers must have full CDD completed
- No further transition relief available

## 9. COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST FOR SMALL ACCOUNTING FIRMS

### Pre-Registration (Before March 31, 2026)

- [ ] Identify which services trigger AML obligations
- [ ] Review existing client list for designated service clients
- [ ] Assign AML/CTF Compliance Officer
- [ ] Begin developing AML/CTF program
- [ ] Assess technology/software needs
- [ ] Budget for compliance costs

### Registration (By March 31, 2026)

- [ ] Enroll with AUSTRAC
- [ ] Obtain registration confirmation
- [ ] Complete AUSTRAC online training modules

### Program Development (By July 1, 2026)

- [ ] Complete Part A Risk Assessment
- [ ] Develop Part B AML/CTF policies and procedures
- [ ] Create customer risk rating system
- [ ] Develop CDD templates and forms
- [ ] Create record keeping system
- [ ] Develop staff training materials
- [ ] Implement transaction monitoring process
- [ ] Create suspicious matter reporting procedure

### Staff Training (By July 1, 2026)

- [ ] Train all staff on AML/CTF obligations
- [ ] Train staff on customer identification procedures
- [ ] Train staff on red flags and suspicious activity
- [ ] Train staff on tipping-off prohibition
- [ ] Document all training provided

### Customer Onboarding (From July 1, 2026)

- [ ] Implement CDD for all new customers
- [ ] Begin transitioning existing customers (3-year plan)
- [ ] Prioritize existing customers by risk
- [ ] Collect and verify customer information
- [ ] Conduct PEP screening
- [ ] Document all CDD procedures

### Ongoing Compliance (From July 1, 2026)

- [ ] Conduct OCDD reviews as scheduled
- [ ] Monitor transactions for suspicious activity
- [ ] Submit SMRs when required
- [ ] Update customer information regularly
- [ ] Review and update AML/CTF program annually
- [ ] Provide ongoing staff training
- [ ] Maintain compliant record keeping
- [ ] Respond to AUSTRAC requests promptly
