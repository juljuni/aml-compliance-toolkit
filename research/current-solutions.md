# Current AML/CTF Compliance Solutions for Australian Accounting Firms

## Overview of Available Solutions

As accounting firms prepare for the July 2026 AML/CTF compliance deadline, several solutions have emerged to help meet obligations. These range from guidance and templates provided by professional bodies to comprehensive commercial software platforms. The right solution depends on firm size, service offerings, risk profile, and budget.

## Professional Body Offerings

### CPA Australia

**Guidance and Resources**:

CPA Australia has been actively supporting members through the transition to AML/CTF regulation, providing educational resources, guidance documents, and policy advocacy.

**Available Resources**:
- **Educational webinars and seminars**: Regular sessions explaining AML/CTF obligations, implementation timelines, and practical compliance strategies
- **Guidance articles**: Published in *INPRACTICE* magazine and on the CPA Australia website, covering topics such as:
  - Overview of the legislative changes and July 2026 deadline
  - Step-by-step implementation guides
  - Risk assessment frameworks
  - Customer due diligence procedures
  - Case studies and examples relevant to accounting practices
- **Member support hotline**: Access to technical support for members with AML/CTF compliance questions
- **Collaboration with AUSTRAC**: CPA Australia, along with CA ANZ and IPA, has made submissions to AUSTRAC calling for clear, sector-specific guidance on threat environments and compliance expectations

**Advocacy**:
CPA Australia has raised concerns with government and AUSTRAC about:
- The compressed implementation timeline
- Need for proportionate obligations recognizing the difference between accounting firms and financial institutions
- Importance of practical, sector-specific guidance rather than generic regulatory materials
- Cost burden on small practices

**Limitations**:
- CPA Australia provides guidance and education but does not offer a turnkey compliance software solution
- Members must still develop their own AML/CTF programs, policies, and procedures
- No technology platform for customer due diligence, monitoring, or reporting
- Firms must separately source software or manage compliance manually

**Pricing**:
- Most guidance and educational resources are available at no additional cost to CPA Australia members as part of standard membership
- Membership fees vary based on membership category (ranging from approximately $400-$900 annually for practicing members)

### Chartered Accountants Australia and New Zealand (CA ANZ)

**Guidance and Resources**:

CA ANZ has developed resources specifically for smaller practices navigating AML/CTF compliance, recognizing that many members operate small or sole practitioner firms with limited compliance infrastructure.

**Available Resources**:
- **SMP (Smaller Member Practices) Resource Centre**: Dedicated section with AML/CTF compliance materials tailored to small firms
- **Implementation guides**: Step-by-step guides covering:
  - Determining whether you're a reporting entity
  - Conducting a money laundering and terrorism financing risk assessment
  - Developing an AML/CTF program
  - Customer identification and verification procedures
  - Ongoing due diligence and monitoring
  - Reporting obligations (SMRs, TTRs, compliance reports)
- **Template documents**: CA ANZ provides members with template AML/CTF programs, customer information forms, risk assessment frameworks, and policy documents that can be customized to individual firms
- **Educational events**: Webinars, workshops, and conference sessions dedicated to AML/CTF compliance
- **Technical support**: Members can contact CA ANZ technical advisory team for guidance on specific compliance questions

**Policy Engagement**:
CA ANZ has actively engaged with government, Treasury, and AUSTRAC to advocate for:
- Proportionate regulation that doesn't impose bank-level compliance burdens on professional service providers
- Extended implementation timelines to allow adequate preparation
- Clear guidance on designated services and what triggers obligations
- Recognition of different risk profiles between accounting services and financial services

**Limitations**:
- Like CPA Australia, CA ANZ provides guidance and templates but not a comprehensive technology solution
- Firms must implement their own systems for customer verification, monitoring, and AUSTRAC reporting
- Templates require customization and firms must ensure they're appropriate for their specific circumstances

**Pricing**:
- Resources available to CA ANZ members as part of standard membership
- Membership fees vary by category and jurisdiction (typically $400-$1,000+ annually for practicing members)

### Institute of Public Accountants (IPA)

**Guidance and Resources**:

IPA provides support to members, particularly focusing on small and regional practices that make up a significant portion of its membership base.

**Available Resources**:
- **AML/CTF compliance hub**: Centralized resource library with fact sheets, guides, and FAQs
- **Educational webinars**: Regular sessions covering compliance requirements and implementation strategies
- **Technical support**: Member access to IPA's technical team for compliance queries
- **Advocacy**: IPA has joined CPA Australia and CA ANZ in making joint submissions to AUSTRAC seeking sector-specific guidance and proportionate regulation

**Focus on Small Firms**:
IPA recognizes that many members operate small practices with limited resources and has tailored its guidance to address:
- Cost-effective compliance strategies
- Leveraging existing client relationship management systems
- Manual compliance processes for firms with low volumes of designated services
- When it's cost-effective to invest in specialized software vs. manual processes

**Limitations**:
- Guidance and education focused, not a technology solution provider
- Members must build their own compliance infrastructure

**Pricing**:
- Resources included in IPA membership (fees typically range from $400-$800 annually depending on membership category)

## Commercial Software Solutions

### TrustEasy

**Overview**:
TrustEasy is an Australian AML/CTF compliance software platform specifically designed for professional services including accounting firms. It provides an end-to-end solution for managing AML/CTF obligations.

**Features**:
- **Customer onboarding workflows**: Guided processes for collecting customer information, identity documents, and beneficial ownership details
- **Electronic verification integration**: Built-in connections to Australian document verification services (DVS), ASIC searches, and electronic identity verification providers
- **Risk assessment tools**: Automated risk scoring based on configurable criteria (customer type, jurisdiction, industry, PEP status, etc.)
- **PEP and sanctions screening**: Integrated screening against global politically exposed person databases and sanctions lists
- **Ongoing monitoring**: Automated periodic rescreening of customers against updated PEP and sanctions lists; transaction monitoring capabilities
- **Document management**: Secure storage of customer records, identification documents, and due diligence evidence
- **Reporting to AUSTRAC**: Tools to assist with preparing SMRs, TTRs, and compliance reports (note: actual submission to AUSTRAC occurs through AUSTRAC's systems, but TrustEasy helps prepare the reports)
- **Audit trail**: Comprehensive logging of all compliance activities for demonstrating compliance to AUSTRAC
- **Training materials**: Included e-learning modules covering AML/CTF obligations
- **AML/CTF program templates**: Customizable program templates aligned with AUSTRAC requirements

**Pricing**:
- **Beginner tier**: Approximately **$1,200 per year** for up to 5 users with low-risk customers and limited volumes
- Higher tiers available for larger firms, higher-risk customer bases, or greater transaction volumes (pricing not publicly disclosed but likely scales to several thousand dollars annually)

**Coverage**:
- Comprehensive coverage of core AML/CTF obligations
- Suitable for small to medium accounting firms
- Designed specifically for Australian regulatory requirements

## Key Considerations When Choosing a Solution

### Firm Size and Transaction Volume

- **Sole practitioners and micro-firms** (1-3 staff, <20 designated service customers/year):
  - May manage compliance manually using professional body templates
  - Entry-level software ($1,200/year) may be cost-effective for efficiency and reducing errors
  - Focus on simple, user-friendly solutions requiring minimal training

- **Small firms** (4-10 staff, 20-100 designated service customers/year):
  - Entry to mid-tier software platforms recommended
  - Balance cost against time savings and compliance risk reduction
  - Look for solutions integrating with existing practice management systems

- **Medium to large firms** (10+ staff, 100+ designated service customers/year):
  - Comprehensive software platform essential
  - Consider solutions with advanced features (transaction monitoring, automated rescreening, detailed reporting)
  - Budget for staff training, system implementation, and ongoing support

### Risk Profile

- **Low-risk firms** (mostly Australian individual and company clients, standard structures, no high-risk jurisdictions):
  - Basic compliance tools may suffice
  - Manual processes with professional body templates viable for very small volumes

- **Medium to high-risk firms** (complex structures, foreign clients, high-risk jurisdictions, PEPs, large transactions):
  - Sophisticated software with enhanced due diligence workflows, sanctions screening, and transaction monitoring critical
  - Consider solutions with strong audit trail and reporting capabilities for demonstrating compliance to AUSTRAC

### Technology Integration

- Firms with established CRM or practice management systems should prioritize solutions that integrate (e.g., StackGo for HubSpot/Salesforce users, IRIS for IRIS practice management users)
- Standalone solutions may create dual data entry burden and workflow friction
- API availability and integration capabilities should be evaluated

### Budget

- Total cost of compliance includes:
  - Software subscription fees
  - Implementation and setup costs
  - Staff training time
  - Ongoing compliance officer time
  - External verification service fees (DVS, ASIC searches, electronic ID verification)
  - Independent review/audit costs (every 2 years)

- For small firms, total annual cost may range from $2,000-$5,000 (software + time + external costs)
- For medium to large firms, costs could exceed $10,000-$50,000 annually depending on scale and complexity

### Recommendation from AUSTRAC

AUSTRAC has advised public practices to **contact AUSTRAC or professional bodies (CPA Australia, CA ANZ, IPA) before purchasing software** to ensure solutions meet regulatory requirements. This suggests:
- Not all commercial solutions are equal in quality or regulatory alignment
- Firms should verify that software adequately addresses their specific obligations
- Professional body consultation can help avoid costly mistakes
