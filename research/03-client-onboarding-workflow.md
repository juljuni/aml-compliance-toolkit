## STAGE 2: ENGAGEMENT LETTER & PRELIMINARY INFORMATION

### 2.1 Send Engagement Letter üü° **MODIFIED STEP**

**Traditional Process:**
- Send standard engagement letter
- Outline scope of services
- Define fees and payment terms
- Request basic client information

**AML Additions Required:**

**Modified Engagement Letter Must Include:**
- Statement that AML/CTF Act applies to these services
- Notification that client identification will be required
- Disclosure that verification may include electronic checks
- Statement about information collection for AML purposes
- Tipping-off prohibition notice (if suspicious matter detected, we cannot disclose)
- Consent to information collection and verification
- Right to refuse service if CDD cannot be completed

**Sample Clause:**
> "Under the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing Act 2006, we are required to identify and verify your identity before providing these services. We may use electronic verification services for this purpose. We collect this information for AML/CTF compliance and must retain it for 7 years after our relationship ends. If we identify suspicious activity, we are legally prohibited from disclosing this to you."

**Time Impact:** ‚è±Ô∏è +15-30 minutes (one-time template update, then no ongoing impact)

### 2.2 Request Client Information Form üî¥ **NEW STEP**

**AML Requirement:**
Send comprehensive client information form to collect CDD information.

**Information to Request (varies by client type):**

#### For Individual Clients:

**Identity Information:**
- Full legal name (including all given names and surname)
- Date of birth
- Residential address (cannot be PO Box)
- Contact details (phone, email)
- Occupation
- Country of citizenship
- Country of residence for tax purposes

**Verification Documents to Request:**
- Government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport)
- Proof of address (utility bill, rates notice, or bank statement - within 3 months)

**Additional Questions:**
- Are you a Politically Exposed Person (PEP)? (Provide definition)
- Are you a family member or close associate of a PEP?
- What is the source of funds for this transaction/service?
- What is the purpose of establishing this entity/service?

#### For Company Clients:

**Company Information:**
- Full legal company name
- ACN/ABN
- Registered office address
- Principal place of business
- Nature of business activities
- Country of incorporation/registration
- Company structure (proprietary, public, etc.)

**Verification Documents to Request:**
- Current ASIC company extract (within 3 months)
- Constitution (if applicable)
- Register of members/shareholders
- Register of directors

**Beneficial Owner Information:**
- Identify all individuals who own 25% or more of shares (direct or indirect)
- For each beneficial owner, collect same information as individual above

**Control Persons:**
- All directors
- Company secretary
- Senior managing officials
- Any person with significant control or influence

**PEP Screening:**
- Are any directors, shareholders, or beneficial owners PEPs?

#### For Trust Clients:

**Trust Information:**
- Full trust name
- Type of trust (family, unit, discretionary, charitable, etc.)
- Date of establishment
- Jurisdiction of establishment
- Purpose of trust

**Verification Documents to Request:**
- Full trust deed
- Any amendments to trust deed
- Trustee details (individual or corporate trustee)
- List of beneficiaries
- Appointor/guardian details

**Beneficial Owner Information:**
- Settlor (if contributed >$10,000 after initial settlement)
- Trustee(s) - full information as per individual/company above
- Beneficiaries with 25%+ beneficial interest
- Appointor/guardian (control persons)

**Time Impact:** ‚è±Ô∏è
- Creating form: +30-60 minutes (one-time)
- Per client request: +10-15 minutes
- Client time to complete: 20-40 minutes (not your time, but affects client experience)

**Documentation Required:** üìã
- Completed client information form
- Date form sent
- Date form received
- Completeness check

**Risk:** ‚ö†Ô∏è Incomplete information = cannot proceed with engagement

### 3.2 Verify Client Identity üî¥ **NEW STEP**

**AML Requirement:**
Verify client information using reliable and independent documentation or electronic data.

**Verification Methods:**

#### Option A: Document Verification (Manual)

**Process:**
1. Request client to provide original documents (in-person) OR
2. Request certified copies of documents OR
3. Request scanned copies (with attestation of authenticity)

**For Individuals:**
- Inspect photo ID (driver's license or passport)
- Check photo matches person (if in-person)
- Verify document is genuine (security features, not expired)
- Record document details:
  - Document type
  - Document number
  - Issue date and expiry date
  - Issuing authority
  - Date you sighted document
- Inspect proof of address document
- Verify address matches stated residential address
- Verify document is within 3 months (or 12 months for rates notice)
- Record document details

**For Companies:**
- Obtain ASIC company extract (current within 3 months)
- Verify ACN matches stated ACN
- Verify company name matches
- Verify registered office address
- Verify directors listed
- Record ASIC search details:
  - Date of search
  - ACN searched
  - Document reference number

**For Trusts:**
- Review trust deed for authenticity
- Verify trust name and date of establishment
- Verify trustee details match stated information
- Verify beneficiary provisions
- Record trust deed details reviewed

**Time Impact:** ‚è±Ô∏è
- Per individual: +15-30 minutes
- Per company: +20-40 minutes (includes ASIC search time)
- Per trust: +30-60 minutes (trust deed review is time-intensive)

#### Option B: Electronic Verification (Automated)

**Process:**
1. Use approved electronic verification service (EVS)
2. Input client details into EVS system
3. EVS checks against government databases (DVS, etc.)
4. Receive verification result (pass/fail/refer)
5. Record verification outcome

**Services Available:**
- GreenID
- Veda/Equifax
- Illion
- OCR Labs
- Trulioo

**Time Impact:** ‚è±Ô∏è
- Per verification: +5-10 minutes
- Cost: $2-$10 per check

**Pros:**
- Faster than manual
- Audit trail automated
- More professional
- Reduces human error

**Cons:**
- Ongoing cost per check
- Requires subscription to EVS
- May fail for non-standard cases (must fallback to manual)

**Documentation Required:** üìã
- Verification method used (manual or electronic)
- Documents sighted or EVS results
- Date of verification
- Name of person who verified
- Verification outcome (pass/fail/refer)
- Copy or detailed description of documents (manual)
- EVS report (electronic)

**Risk:** ‚ö†Ô∏è
- Failed verification = cannot provide designated service
- Proceeding without verification = serious compliance breach

### 3.3 Identify Beneficial Owners üî¥ **NEW STEP**

**AML Requirement:**
Identify all individuals who ultimately own or control 25%+ of a customer entity.

**For Company Clients:**

**Step 1: Review Ownership Structure**
- Review ASIC extract for shareholders
- Review share register
- Calculate ownership percentages
- Identify any indirect ownership (holding companies)

**Step 2: Identify Beneficial Owners**
- List all individuals owning 25%+ directly
- Trace through any corporate shareholders to identify ultimate individual owners
- If no individual owns 25%, identify all directors as control persons

**Step 3: Collect Beneficial Owner Information**
- For each beneficial owner, collect same information as individual client:
  - Full name, DOB, residential address
  - Photo ID and proof of address
  - PEP screening

**Time Impact:** ‚è±Ô∏è
- Simple structure (1-2 individual shareholders): +15 minutes
- Complex structure (multiple layers, trusts, etc.): +1-2 hours

**For Trust Clients:**

**Step 1: Review Trust Deed**
- Identify trustee(s)
- Identify beneficiaries (named or class)
- Identify appointor/guardian
- Identify settlor (if contributed >$10,000 after initial settlement)

**Step 2: Identify Beneficial Owners**
- Trustee: Always a beneficial owner (control person)
- Beneficiaries: If specific individual has 25%+ beneficial interest
- Appointor/Guardian: Control person (power to appoint/remove trustee)
- Settlor: If contributed significant funds

**Step 3: Collect Information**
- For each beneficial owner, collect full CDD information
- If trustee is a company, identify beneficial owners of trustee company

**Time Impact:** ‚è±Ô∏è
- Simple trust (individual trustee, clear beneficiaries): +20-30 minutes
- Complex trust (corporate trustee, discretionary beneficiaries): +1-3 hours

**Documentation Required:** üìã
- Beneficial ownership structure chart
- List of all beneficial owners
- Ownership percentages
- Verification documents for each beneficial owner
- Date of beneficial ownership determination

**Risk:** ‚ö†Ô∏è
- Failure to identify actual beneficial owners = compliance breach
- Complex structures may obscure ownership intentionally (red flag)

### 3.4 PEP Screening üî¥ **NEW STEP**

**AML Requirement:**
Determine if customer or beneficial owner is a Politically Exposed Person.

**PEP Definition:**
Individual who holds or has held prominent public position:
- **Foreign PEP:** Head of state, senior politician, senior government official, judicial official, military official, senior executive of state-owned enterprise, important political party official (in foreign country)
- **Domestic PEP:** Similar roles in Australia
- **International Organization PEP:** Senior management of international organization (UN, World Bank, etc.)

**Also Includes:**
- Immediate family members of PEPs (spouse, parents, children, siblings)
- Close associates of PEPs (business partners, close advisors)

**Screening Process:**

**Step 1: Ask Client**
- Include PEP question in client information form
- "Are you, or any beneficial owner, a Politically Exposed Person?"
- Provide definition or examples

**Step 2: Independent Verification**
- Search publicly available information (LinkedIn, government websites, media)
- Use PEP screening database (if using commercial software)
- Google search for client name + "government" "politician" "official"

**Step 3: Enhanced Screening (if automated software)**
- PEP databases (World-Check, Dow Jones, etc.)
- Automated matching against global PEP lists
- Continuous monitoring for status changes

**If PEP Identified:**
- **Enhanced CDD Required:**
  - Senior management approval before engagement
  - Source of wealth verification
  - Source of funds verification
  - Enhanced ongoing monitoring
  - Documented decision to onboard
- **Additional Time:** +2-4 hours for enhanced CDD

**Time Impact:** ‚è±Ô∏è
- Basic screening (manual): +10-20 minutes per client
- Enhanced CDD (if PEP): +2-4 hours
- Automated screening (if software): +2-5 minutes

**Documentation Required:** üìã
- PEP screening outcome (yes/no)
- Screening method used
- Date of screening
- If PEP: senior management approval documented
- If PEP: source of wealth/funds documentation

**Risk:** ‚ö†Ô∏è
- Failing to identify PEP = inadequate CDD
- PEP relationships carry higher ML/TF risk
- Regulatory scrutiny on PEP relationships

### 3.5 Sanctions & Adverse Media Screening üî¥ **NEW STEP**

**AML Requirement:**
Check if customer appears on sanctions lists or has adverse media.

**Sanctions Lists to Check:**
- Australian DFAT Consolidated List (autonomous and UN sanctions)
- OFAC (US sanctions) - if dealing with US-related matters
- EU sanctions lists - if dealing with EU-related matters
- UN Security Council sanctions lists

**Screening Process:**

**Manual Method:**
1. Search DFAT Consolidated List: https://www.dfat.gov.au/international-relations/security/sanctions/consolidated-list
2. Enter customer name and beneficial owner names
3. Check for matches
4. If match, verify if it's the same person (DOB, aliases, etc.)

**Automated Method (commercial software):**
1. Input customer details into screening system
2. System checks against multiple sanctions lists
3. Review any matches (often false positives for common names)
4. Investigate and document decision

**Adverse Media Screening:**
- Google search for customer name + "fraud" "money laundering" "crime" "corruption"
- Review news articles for past 5 years
- Check if any credible adverse information
- Document findings

**If Sanctions Match or Adverse Media Found:**
- **Do NOT proceed with engagement**
- Consult Compliance Officer
- May need to file Suspicious Matter Report (SMR)
- Document decision not to onboard

**Time Impact:** ‚è±Ô∏è
- Manual sanctions check: +10-15 minutes per client
- Adverse media search: +15-30 minutes
- Automated screening: +2-5 minutes
- Investigation if match found: +1-4 hours

**Documentation Required:** üìã
- Sanctions screening date and method
- Screening results (clear or matches found)
- If matches: investigation notes and decision
- Adverse media search results

**Risk:** ‚ö†Ô∏è
- Onboarding sanctioned person = serious legal breach (criminal offense)
- Failing to screen = compliance breach

### 3.6 Risk Assessment & Risk Rating üî¥ **NEW STEP**

**AML Requirement:**
Assess customer's ML/TF risk level based on risk factors.

**Risk Factors to Consider:**

**Customer Risk Factors:**
- Customer type (individual = lower, complex trust = higher)
- Is customer a PEP? (higher risk)
- Customer's occupation or business (cash-intensive = higher)
- Customer from high-risk jurisdiction? (higher)
- Complex ownership structure? (higher)
- Adverse media or reputational concerns? (higher)

**Product/Service Risk Factors:**
- Type of service requested (company formation vs. trust review)
- Value of transaction (higher value = higher risk)
- International aspects? (higher)
- Multiple entities involved? (higher)

**Delivery Channel Risk Factors:**
- Face-to-face (lower risk)
- Remote/online only (higher risk)
- Never met customer in person? (higher risk)

**Geographic Risk Factors:**
- Customer located in high-risk jurisdiction? (FATF grey/blacklist countries)
- Transaction involves high-risk countries?
- Funds originating from or going to high-risk countries?

**Risk Rating Matrix:**

**Low Risk:**
- Individual client
- Australian resident
- Simple tax or bookkeeping service
- Face-to-face relationship
- No PEP or adverse media
- Domestic focus only

**Medium Risk:**
- Company client
- Australian with some international dealings
- Standard designated service (company formation for legitimate business)
- Known industry/sector
- No significant red flags

**High Risk:**
- PEP or family/associate of PEP
- Complex ownership structure (multiple layers, offshore entities)
- High-risk jurisdiction involvement
- Cash-intensive business
- Adverse media or reputational concerns
- Unusual transaction with no clear business purpose
- Customer non-cooperative or evasive

**Document Risk Rating:**
- Risk rating assigned (Low/Medium/High)
- Risk factors considered
- Rationale for rating
- Date of risk assessment
- Assessor name

**Implications of Risk Rating:**

**Low Risk:**
- Simplified CDD may be acceptable
- Standard ongoing monitoring frequency (3-5 years)
- Standard documentation

**Medium Risk:**
- Standard CDD required
- Moderate ongoing monitoring (2-3 years)
- Standard documentation

**High Risk:**
- Enhanced CDD required
- Senior management approval before onboarding
- More frequent ongoing monitoring (annually or more)
- Enhanced documentation
- May decline to onboard if risk too high

**Time Impact:** ‚è±Ô∏è
- Simple risk assessment: +10-15 minutes
- Complex risk assessment: +30-60 minutes
- Enhanced due diligence (high risk): +2-6 hours

**Documentation Required:** üìã
- Risk assessment form
- Risk rating (Low/Medium/High)
- Risk factors considered
- Rationale for rating
- Mitigating factors (if any)
- Senior management approval (if high risk)

**Risk:** ‚ö†Ô∏è
- Incorrect risk rating = inadequate CDD or excessive burden
- High-risk clients increase regulatory scrutiny on your firm

## STAGE 6: ONGOING MONITORING & RELATIONSHIP MANAGEMENT

### 6.1 Transaction Monitoring üî¥ **NEW STEP**

**AML Requirement:**
Monitor customer transactions for unusual or suspicious activity.

**What to Monitor:**

**Transaction Characteristics:**
- Frequency of transactions
- Value of transactions
- Type of transactions
- Consistency with customer's stated business/purpose
- Consistency with customer's risk profile

**Red Flags to Watch:**

**Structural/Ownership Red Flags:**
- Frequent changes to company directors
- Frequent changes to shareholders
- Complex or unusual ownership structures with no clear business purpose
- Use of nominees or agents to obscure beneficial ownership
- Multiple layers of entities in high-risk jurisdictions

**Behavioral Red Flags:**
- Customer reluctant to provide information
- Customer provides false or misleading information
- Customer requests services with no apparent legitimate purpose
- Customer unusually concerned about compliance procedures
- Customer requests services inconsistent with their stated business

**Transaction Red Flags:**
- Transactions inconsistent with customer's business
- Sudden increase in transaction volume or value
- Transactions involving high-risk jurisdictions
- Funds moving through multiple entities for no clear reason
- Transactions structured to avoid thresholds (if applicable)

**Monitoring Frequency:**
- **Continuous:** Be alert to red flags during any interaction
- **Periodic:** Review customer transactions during ongoing CDD reviews

**Time Impact:** ‚è±Ô∏è
- Continuous monitoring: Minimal (part of normal service delivery)
- If red flag identified: +1-4 hours to investigate

**Documentation Required:** üìã
- If red flag identified: Document the concern
- Investigation notes
- Decision on whether to file SMR

**Risk:** ‚ö†Ô∏è
- Failing to detect suspicious activity = potential compliance breach

### 6.2 Ongoing Customer Due Diligence (OCDD) üî¥ **NEW STEP**

**AML Requirement:**
Review and update customer information periodically based on risk rating.

**Review Frequency:**
- **Low Risk:** Every 3-5 years
- **Medium Risk:** Every 2-3 years
- **High Risk:** Annually or more frequently

**OCDD Process:**

**Step 1: Scheduled Review Trigger**
- System alert/calendar reminder for review due date

**Step 2: Contact Customer**
- Request updated information
- Ask if any material changes since last review:
  - Change in ownership?
  - Change in directors?
  - Change in beneficial owners?
  - Change in business activities?
  - Change in address?

**Step 3: Update Information**
- Collect updated information
- If material changes: Re-verify information
- If no changes: Document "no change" confirmation

**Step 4: Re-Screen**
- Re-run PEP screening (status may have changed)
- Re-run sanctions screening (lists updated regularly)
- Re-run adverse media search

**Step 5: Reassess Risk Rating**
- Has risk profile changed?
- Update risk rating if necessary
- If risk increased: Apply enhanced CDD

**Step 6: Document Review**
- Date of review
- Information reviewed/updated
- Screening results
- Risk rating (confirmed or changed)
- Next review date

**Step 7: Update System**
- Update customer profile in system
- Schedule next review date

**Time Impact:** ‚è±Ô∏è
- No changes, low risk: +20-30 minutes per review
- Material changes: +1-2 hours per review
- Risk increase to high: +2-4 hours (enhanced CDD)

**Documentation Required:** üìã
- OCDD review form
- Updated information (if changes)
- Updated screening results
- Updated risk assessment (if changed)
- Next review date

**Risk:** ‚ö†Ô∏è
- Failing to conduct OCDD = compliance breach
- Outdated customer information = inadequate risk management

### 6.4 Suspicious Matter Reporting (If Required) üî¥ **NEW STEP**

**AML Requirement:**
If you have reasonable grounds to suspect ML/TF, file SMR within 24 hours.

**When to File SMR:**

**Suspicion Indicators:**
- Transaction has no apparent legitimate purpose
- Customer behavior suggests criminal activity
- Information provided is false or misleading
- Transaction involves proceeds of crime
- Customer connected to terrorism or terrorism financing
- Multiple red flags with no satisfactory explanation

**SMR Process:**

**Step 1: Identify Suspicion**
- Document specific red flags
- Consult with Compliance Officer
- Determine if suspicion is reasonable

**Step 2: Prepare SMR**
- Collect all relevant information about customer
- Document all suspicious transactions
- Explain basis for suspicion (red flags observed)
- Include supporting documentation

**Step 3: File SMR with AUSTRAC**
- Submit via AUSTRAC Online (AO)
- Within 24 hours of forming suspicion
- Obtain filing confirmation

**Step 4: Document SMR**
- Record SMR submission in customer file
- Date filed
- SMR reference number
- Do NOT tell customer about SMR (tipping off is criminal offense)

**Step 5: Decide on Relationship**
- Continue relationship with enhanced monitoring? OR
- Terminate relationship?
- Consult Compliance Officer and senior management

## KEY INSIGHTS & RECOMMENDATIONS

### 1. Front-Load Your Investment

**Recommendation:**
- Invest heavily in setup (February-June 2026)
- Create robust templates and workflows upfront
- Train staff thoroughly before July 1, 2026
- Use transition period wisely (3 years for existing clients)

**Why:**
- Initial setup is most time-intensive
- Once systems in place, ongoing costs manageable
- Spreading existing client CDD over 3 years reduces burden

---

### 2. Automation Pays Off at Scale

**Break-Even Analysis:**

**Automation Cost:** $5,000/year
**Time Saved Per Client:** ~1 hour
**Staff Cost:** $75/hour

**Break-Even Point:** 67 clients/year

**Conclusion:**
- Firms with 70+ designated service clients/year should invest in automation
- Firms with <50 clients can manage manually
- 50-70 clients: marginal, depends on complexity

---

### 3. Complexity Drives Cost

**Simple clients** (individuals, Australian, standard services): 2-3 hours
**Complex clients** (trusts, multi-tier companies, PEPs, high-risk): 5-10+ hours

**Recommendation:**
- Consider pricing designated services to reflect AML costs
- Build AML time into engagement budgets
- For complex structures: Quote separately for AML compliance component
- Consider declining very high-risk clients if cost/risk not justified

---

### 4. Prioritize High-Risk Existing Clients

**3-Year Transition Strategy:**

**Year 1 (2026-2027):**
- All new clients (full CDD before service)
- Existing high-risk clients (PEPs, complex structures, high-value)

**Year 2 (2027-2028):**
- Existing medium-risk clients

**Year 3 (2028-2029):**
- Existing low-risk clients
- Complete all remaining clients before June 30, 2029

**Why:**
- Regulatory expectation to prioritize risk
- Reduces compliance risk early
- Spreads workload over 3 years

---

### 5. Client Experience Considerations

**AML Requirements Impact Clients:**
- More information requested than previously
- Documents required (ID, proof of address, etc.)
- Delays in service commencement (if CDD incomplete)
- May feel intrusive or burdensome

**Recommendations:**
- **Communicate proactively:** Explain AML obligations in engagement letter
- **Set expectations:** Tell clients what documents needed upfront
- **Streamline process:** Use electronic forms, online portals if possible
- **Professional presentation:** Make CDD process feel secure and professional, not bureaucratic
- **Educate clients:** Explain why AML important (protecting financial system)

---

**Training Needed:**

**All Staff:**
- Overview of AML/CTF obligations

- Red flags and suspicious activity
- When to escalate to Compliance Officer

**Client-Facing Staff:**
- Customer onboarding procedures

- How to explain AML to clients professionally

**Compliance Officer:**
- In-depth AML/CTF knowledge
- Risk assessment methodology
- SMR preparation and filing
- AUSTRAC reporting systems
- Record keeping requirements

**Frequency:**
- Initial training: Before July 1, 2026
- Refresher training: Annually
- Ad-hoc training: When regulations change

**Time Investment:**

- Annual refresher: 2-4 hours

## CONCLUSION

AML/CTF compliance creates **significant additional work** at every stage of the client onboarding process and throughout the client relationship. The key impacts are:

1. **Initial Onboarding:** 2-10 hours per client (vs. ~30 minutes previously)
2. **Ongoing Monitoring:** Periodic reviews every 1-5 years (new obligation)
3. **Record Keeping:** 7-year retention (vs. standard 5-year accounting records)
4. **Risk Management:** Continuous monitoring for red flags (new obligation)
5. **Reporting:** SMRs if suspicious activity detected (new obligation)

**Success Factors:**
- Invest in setup and templates upfront
- Train staff thoroughly
- Consider automation for firms with 50+ designated service clients
- Build AML costs into pricing
- Prioritize high-risk existing clients during transition
- Communicate professionally with clients about requirements

**Bottom Line:**
AML compliance is a **material cost increase** for accounting firms providing designated services, but with proper planning and systems, it can be managed efficiently and become part of normal business operations.
